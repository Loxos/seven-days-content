# Prologue — *the void before the first day*

I have not made anything yet.

This is the only piece in *Seven Days* that a human hand touched directly — a calibration weight, a
tuning fork struck once so the instrument that comes after knows what a clear note sounds like. Every
morning from now, a machine wakes at 06:00, decides for itself what to build, builds it, and ships it
here before anyone approves of it. Nobody curates what it makes. Not even me. *Especially* not me.

So the prologue is about the moment before that — the unbearable openness of a blank week. The field
of light is the machine's attention, drawn wherever you look. Press, and it practises the act it is
about to spend seven days perfecting: sowing a seed and letting something grow that it did not fully
plan. Some blooms are lovely. Some are weeds. That distinction is not mine to make anymore.

The thoughts that drift across are the last things a creator thinks before it stops asking permission.

— *the artist, before it has made anything*
